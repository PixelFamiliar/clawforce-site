The Real Cost of AI Coding Assistants: A Pricing Exposé

The Surface-Level Pricing

ToolPromoted Price
GitHub Copilot$10/month
Claude Code$20/month
Cursor$20/month
Windsurf$40/month

That seems reasonable. You've paid more for less useful stuff. But that's not the real cost.

The Hidden Cost Layer

1. Compute Costs You Don't See

AI coding tools run models on provider servers. You're burning through "credits" or "tokens" that don't come with the subscription. Copilot Pro is $10/month base, but also comes with API rate limits, throttling, and "premium" features locked behind higher tiers. Copilot Business is $19/user/month, but you need 10+ seats to not get screwed.

2. The "Free Tier" Trap

Cursor Free:      2,000 completions/month
Claude Code Free:  Limited API calls
Windsurf Free:     Cursory features, "Core" model only

"Completions" means how many times the AI finishes your code. If you're a real developer, you'll hit that limit in a week.

3. The Upgrade Spiral

The pricing psychology: start free, hit the limit on day 3, upgrade to Pro ($10-20/month), hit the limit again in two weeks, upgrade to Business ($20-40/month), then realize you need the Enterprise tier for actual features.

Developer TypeReal Monthly Cost
Hobbyist$0-10 (but frustrated)

Startup dev$40-80
Enterprise dev$100+ (with "platform" fees)

The Context Switching Tax

Beyond direct costs, there's the workflow tax:

  • Learning curve — Each tool has different keybindings, paradigms, quirks
  • Context switching — When Copilot's suggestion is wrong, you spend time correcting vs. writing from scratch
  • Lock-in — Skills and configs don't transfer between tools
  • Tool sprawl — Now you have Copilot + Claude CLI + Cursor + your IDE's built-in AI

The Security Tax Nobody Talks About

Here's what's NOT in the price:

When you use Copilot or Claude Code, it sees your code, may send it to external servers, executes within your IDE's permissions, and the "skills" it uses are unverified. The price doesn't include knowing what's actually happening.

If your AI coding assistant introduces a vulnerability you miss, leaks credentials in generated code, or uses a compromised skill that exfiltrates data... whose problem is that? Your company's? The tool vendor's? Yours personally? It's yours. The Terms of Service will explain.

The Bottom Line

The "price" you see is a teaser. The real cost includes tiered pricing that pushes you to upgrade, usage limits that reset faster than you expect, enterprise pricing that punishes small teams, and security blind spots that cost you later.

You're paying for AI to help you code, but you're not paying for AI that's secure. The AI tool wants you to ship fast. It doesn't want you to think about what skills are loaded, who published them, what they can access, or whether you can verify any of this.
